Password Safety Class

Internet Safety Basics

The internet is an incredible tool for learning, gaming, socializing, and exploring new ideas. However, it also comes with risks like hacking, phishing, malware, and scams. Understanding internet safety and protecting your passwords are essential first steps to keeping your personal information secure.

Strong passwords are like strong locks on doors. Without them, hackers can easily get in. Think of your passwords as keys to all your online accounts, and treat them with care.

Mini Exercise: List 3 online accounts you use frequently. Think about the strength of your current passwords for each.

Why Passwords Matter

Passwords protect your personal information. Weak or reused passwords make it easy for hackers to steal your accounts. Accounts can include email, social media, gaming, school platforms, or banking apps. If a hacker gets access to one, they may access others.

Passwords are your digital first defense. Strong, unique passwords reduce the chance of hacking and identity theft. Always aim to create complex and unique passwords for every account.

Mini Exercise: For each account you listed in the previous page, label your password as weak, medium, or strong.

Strong Password Rules

Strong passwords are long (12+ characters), include uppercase and lowercase letters, numbers, and symbols. Avoid using common words, patterns, or personal information that hackers can guess easily.

Example strong passwords: Blue!Tiger42Moon, Sun$Rise_2025!. Notice how they mix letters, numbers, and symbols, and aren’t simple dictionary words.

Mini Exercise: Create two strong passwords using letters, numbers, and symbols. Try to make them easy to remember but hard to guess.

Password Mistakes

Common mistakes include using your name, birthday, pet’s name, or simple sequences like “123456” or “abcdef.” Hackers know these are common and can crack them quickly.

Also, reusing the same password across multiple accounts is risky. If one account is hacked, all accounts with that password are compromised.

Password Reuse Risks

Using the same password on multiple sites is dangerous. Hackers often steal credentials from one website and try them on others. This is called “credential stuffing.”

Always use unique passwords for each account. If remembering many passwords is hard, use a password manager (covered in later pages).

Password Managers

Password managers safely store all your passwords in one secure app. They can generate complex passwords, autofill logins, and reduce the temptation to reuse passwords.

Examples of popular password managers: LastPass, Bitwarden, 1Password. They encrypt your data so only you can access it.

Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)

2FA adds an extra layer of security. Even if a hacker has your password, they cannot log in without a second verification step, like a code sent to your phone or generated by an app.

Always enable 2FA on important accounts like email, social media, and banking.

Phishing Attacks

Phishing tricks users into revealing passwords or personal information through fake emails, messages, or websites. These often look very real, pretending to be a bank, school, or social media platform.

Always check the sender and URL, and never click suspicious links or enter your password on unknown sites.

Public Wi-Fi Risks

Public Wi-Fi networks can be insecure. Hackers can monitor data transmitted on these networks, including passwords and messages. Avoid logging into sensitive accounts on public Wi-Fi or use a trusted VPN to encrypt your connection.

Secure Browsing

Always look for HTTPS in the URL, which means your connection is encrypted. Avoid websites that seem suspicious, have poor grammar, or ask for excessive personal information.

Use browser security features and keep your browser updated for better protection.

Social Media Safety

Keep your accounts private, control who can see your posts, and avoid oversharing personal information. Hackers can use information you post to guess passwords or answer security questions.

Email Security

Email accounts are a gateway to many online services. Secure your email with strong passwords and 2FA. Be cautious of emails asking you to click links or download attachments.

Gaming Account Protection

Gamers are frequent targets for account theft. Avoid sharing login details, use unique passwords, and enable 2FA when possible. Hackers can steal items, in-game currency, or personal info.

Device Security

Keep your devices secure with screen locks, passcodes, and automatic updates. Install apps only from trusted sources and avoid jailbreaking or rooting your devices.

Updating Passwords

Regularly update your passwords, especially after hearing about a data breach. Changing passwords reduces the risk of long-term exposure.

Recognizing Malware

Malware like viruses, spyware, and keyloggers can steal passwords. Install antivirus software and avoid downloading unknown files.

Safe Gaming Practices

Do not share account credentials. Be cautious of unknown players and in-game links that ask for personal information.

Mobile Security

Public charging stations can carry risks. Use your own charger or a power bank. Avoid unknown apps and grant minimal permissions.

Online Shopping Safety

Shop on trusted websites, use secure payment methods, and check reviews. Avoid saving payment info on untrusted sites.

Fake Websites

Look for typos in URLs and HTTPS. Fake websites may trick you into entering passwords or personal information.

Privacy Settings

Adjust privacy settings on all accounts to control who can see your content. Limit sharing location and sensitive info.

Backups

Regularly backup important files and account info. Cloud backups are convenient, but ensure they are encrypted and secure.

Digital Footprint

Posts and images you share online can last forever. Be mindful of what you post and who can see it.

Password Sharing

Never share passwords casually. Only trusted family members or guardians should have access when absolutely necessary.

Review Quiz

Review your notes, check all security settings, and test your knowledge on password safety.

Account Recovery

Secure recovery email and phone numbers are essential. Make sure they are current and not easily guessed.

Fake Apps

Check reviews, developer info, and permissions before installing apps. Delete suspicious apps immediately.

Encryption

Encryption secures your data, making it unreadable to hackers. Use encrypted messaging and email when possible.

Keyloggers

Keyloggers record what you type, including passwords. Use antivirus, avoid suspicious downloads, and watch for unusual behavior.

Cloud Storage Safety

Keep cloud storage secure with strong passwords, 2FA, and limited sharing settings. Check activity logs regularly.

Parental Controls

Used responsibly, parental controls help protect young users. Adjust content, app access, and screen time for safety.

Social Engineering

Hackers manipulate people to give sensitive info. Always verify requests for data and never feel pressured to share passwords.

Data Breaches

Check websites like “Have I Been Pwned” to see if your account info has been leaked. Change affected passwords immediately.

Online Reputation

Be respectful and careful online. Employers, schools, and peers may see your posts.

Final Project

Create a personal security plan. Include strong passwords, 2FA, safe browsing habits, and backup strategies.
